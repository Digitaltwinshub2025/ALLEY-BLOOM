Water Alley Transformation

Side-by-side comparison of existing conditions and proposed design interventions for Alley 3 in Pico-Union, Los Angeles.

Overall Site Comparison

Water Alley is a 300-foot service corridor between W 11th and W 12th Street. Three integrated interventions -- shade structures, community murals, and urban farming -- address heat exposure, cultural identity, and food access.

BEFORE

Exposed asphalt corridor / no shade, greenery, or community infrastructure

Intervention-by-Intervention

Each intervention targets specific zones based on existing conditions and spatial compatibility.

Intervention 1

Shade Structures -- Areas B & C

Before: Exposed corridor at Area B / 120 F+ ground temps in summer

After: Overhead solar canopy + trellises / 85% shade coverage

Tensile fabric canopy with integrated solar panels mounted 12-15 ft above ground. Vertical trellises support native climbing plants. Water-themed medallions reinforce alley identity.

View full Shade Structures page
Intervention 2

Community Murals -- Areas C & D

Before: Blank concrete walls / graffiti and surface damage

After: Water-themed murals / community identity and wayfinding

Wall-based cultural infrastructure transforms blank concrete into community landmarks. Water-themed imagery connects to alley identity. Two existing murals preserved; 12 walls prepared for new designs.

View full Community Murals page
Intervention 3

Urban Farming -- Areas A & B

Before: Cracked pavement / no productive ground use

After: Raised bed containers / fresh produce for 20 families

Modular raised bed containers along alley edges produce fresh vegetables for neighborhood families. Pico-Union is classified as a food desert -- this intervention addresses food access within steps of residents' homes.
