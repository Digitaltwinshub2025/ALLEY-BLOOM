INFRASTRUCTURE INTERVENTION

Shade Structure System

Overhead canopy infrastructure for thermal regulation and solar energy generation

Enter 3D Shade Simulation

A.Existing Conditions

Exposed asphalt corridor / no shade or infrastructure

Water Alley is a 300-foot service corridor with exposed asphalt that absorbs and radiates heat, reaching ground temperatures over 120°F in summer. The space has no shade, greenery, or infrastructure for community use.

B.Intervention Description

Integrated shade system in Alley 3 / solar canopy + trellises + medallions

Concept Reference

Generic solar canopy system for reference

Integrated overhead canopy system combining solar panels, structural trellises, and decorative medallions. Designed for thermal regulation, energy generation, and visual identity.

Zone-Specific Implementation

Shade structures are applied to Areas B and C, where alley width (18-20 feet) and wall conditions support overhead structural installation.

C.Infrastructure Foundation

Shade structures integrate with documented fence and wall repairs completed across 15 sections in Areas A, B, and C. This foundation work provides structural mounting points and reinforced surfaces for overhead canopy systems.

Documented Repair Work

Area B - Projects 4 & 5

  • 6 sections of wall and gate restoration
  • Large stucco wall repairs (~230-250 sq ft)
  • Metal gate repainting with rust protection
  • Driveway gate structural reinforcement
  • Iron fence restoration with concrete base work

Area C - Projects 6 & 7

  • 9 sections of iron gate and fence repairs
  • Concrete wall surface preparation
  • Rust removal and protective coating application
  • Structural column patching and reinforcement
  • Wall cleaning and anti-graffiti sealant

Total Foundation: 15 documented sections | ~1,400 sq ft of restored surfaces | Metal, iron, and concrete infrastructure prepared for shade system integration

D.System Components

1

Overhead Solar Canopy

Tensile fabric with integrated PV panels, 12-15 ft above ground. Provides 85% shade and generates 5kW solar power.

2

Vertical Trellises

Wall-mounted steel panels supporting native climbing plants. Creates living green walls without consuming ground space.

3

Water Medallions

Decorative marine life medallions on posts and walls. Creates visual landmarks and reinforces Water Alley identity.
