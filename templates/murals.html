C.Cultural Foundation

Community murals build on documented preservation work completed in Area C, Project 7. Two existing murals were restored with UV protection, graffiti removal, and surface sealing, establishing the cultural foundation for expanded mural programming.

Documented Preservation Work

Project 7, Section 3

  • Concrete wall with painted mural (~165-180 sq ft)
  • Graffiti removal and wall washing
  • UV-protective clear coat application
  • Acrylic paint touch-ups for faded areas
  • Anti-weathering sealant for longevity

Project 7, Section 5

  • "Respect" mural restoration (~85-100 sq ft)
  • Surface cleaning and tag removal
  • Full mural repainting and touch-ups
  • UV-protective sealant application
  • Anti-graffiti coating for protection

Additional Wall Preparation

12 additional sections across Projects 6, 7, and 8 received graffiti removal, wall cleaning, and anti-graffiti sealant application, creating prepared canvases for new community artwork.

Total Foundation: 2 murals preserved | ~265-280 sq ft of restored artwork | 12 walls prepared for new designs

D.Role in Alley 3

  • Improves visual identity: Transforms blank walls into recognizable cultural landmarks
  • Encourages community ownership: Residents see their heritage reflected in public space
  • Creates landmarks for navigation: Murals serve as wayfinding elements along the corridor
  • Reduces perception of neglect: Valued artwork deters tagging and signals community care
