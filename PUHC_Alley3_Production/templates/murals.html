ART SYSTEM

Community Murals

A site-specific mural series designed to transform blank alley walls into cultural landmarks that reflect Water Alley's identity.

Visual concepts tested in the Alley 3 digital twin to explore scale, lighting, and wall placement.

Open Alley 3 Digital Twin

A.Existing Conditions

Blank wall with graffiti / no cultural identity

Water Alley's walls are blank concrete surfaces with accumulated graffiti. The corridor offers no visual identity or cultural expression for the Pico-Union community.

B.Intervention Description

Water-themed mural on Alley 3 wall / marine life imagery

Concept reference (imagery + color palette)

This intervention installs large-scale murals on wall sections along Water Alley. Each mural reinforces the water theme through marine life imagery and community storytelling.

C.Role in Alley 3

  • Improves visual identity: Transforms blank walls into recognizable cultural landmarks
  • Encourages community ownership: Residents see their heritage reflected in public space
  • Creates landmarks for navigation: Murals serve as wayfinding elements along the corridor
  • Reduces perception of neglect: Valued artwork deters tagging and signals community care

D.Digital Twin Behavior

Murals were evaluated for scale, visibility, and lighting in the Alley 3 digital twin.

Before: Blank walls

After: Murals active

  • Texture and material rendering: See how painted surfaces interact with wall textures
